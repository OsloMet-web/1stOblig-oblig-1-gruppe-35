Problem solving

Complicated tasks that require problem solving. These are often tasks that have several approaches and solutions, then you will benefit from brainstorming and getting more ideas. Tasks with a large scope may require many working hours to be completed by the deadline, but more workers will reduce the workload per person and ease the responsibility of the individual. It is also very difficult to check your own task because you can quickly get tunnel vision, this applies to large tasks and problem-solving tasks, where it helps to get an outside perspective before you deliver. It is also nice to be able to work with several segments along the way In the larger tasks to see problems that may arise along the way. Will also help with the motivation factor.

Small grops

When working in a small group, you may come across a problem that you have to pass on or is someone else's work, then your work can stop and this then becomes a bit ineffective if you cannot work because of others. You don't get to work at your own pace or with your own ideas, the fact that you have to compromise can be ineffective compared to working alone when it suits you best. A bad attitude and work ethic can spill over into the group, this can be that someone takes up too little or too much space within the group and can lead to others taking a passive position.
